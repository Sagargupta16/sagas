# Revise a chapter

Run the revision ladder on ONE unpublished chapter (`status: draft` or `revising`). His prose stays his: propose edits as a findings list or, if he says "apply", as minimal diffs.

## Preparation

Read `references/revision.md` and `references/prose-style.md` from the `writing-craft` library. Add `references/pov-tense.md` when filter words show up, `references/dialogue.md` when tags need work.

## The ladder (never skip levels, never start at the bottom)

1. **Story pass**: does the chapter still do its outline job (goal, progress signpost, hook)? If the story is broken, stop here and report; line edits on a broken scene are wasted.
2. **Scene pass**: MRU order (motivation before reaction), scene/sequel balance, paragraphs that repeat a beat already landed.
3. **Line pass**, with the measurable King target -- 2nd draft = 1st draft - 10%:
   - Count words before and after; report both numbers.
   - Cut order: filler phrases, redundant beats, throat-clearing openings, -ly adverbs on tags, thought verbs, filter words (saw/felt/heard/noticed/realized) unless the distance is deliberate (Harnby).
   - R.U.E.: Resist the Urge to Explain -- delete the sentence that explains the emotion the previous sentence already showed (Browne & King).
4. **Read-aloud pass**: flag rhythm faults -- runs of same-length sentences, accidental rhymes, tongue-twisters (Le Guin: the test of a sentence is, does it sound right).
5. **Copyedit**: typos, dialogue punctuation per US convention, `--` never em/en-dash characters, one error type at a time.

## Status transitions

- After a full ladder run: suggest `draft -> revising` or `revising -> final`.
- Publishing (`publishedOn`) is the `publish-chapter` skill's job, never this one's -- its gates (continuity, fanfic checks, mechanical sweep) must not be bypassed by an edit pass.

## Rules

- Published chapters (with `publishedOn`) only get typo/punctuation fixes; anything larger is a new-chapter problem or an author's-note, not a silent rewrite.
- Never cut content just to hit -10% if the chapter is already lean; report the honest number and say why the target doesn't apply.
- Preserve his darlings unless they serve the author instead of the story (Quiller-Couch's actual rule); when proposing such a cut, quote the passage and say exactly that.
