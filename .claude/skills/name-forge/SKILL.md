# Name forge

Names are worldbuilding's most visible surface and the first thing readers judge. Every name request routes through the book's established conventions.

## Preparation

Read the book's `_bible.md` naming ledger and the naming rules in `references/worldbuilding.md`. For chapter/book titles also skim `references/openings-endings.md` (titles are promises too).

## Character and place names

1. Identify the culture/region the name belongs to; use its phonetic signature from the bible. No signature yet -> propose one (2-3 sounds/patterns) and get a yes before generating.
2. Offer 5-8 candidates, each with a one-line rationale (meaning, echo, sound-feel). Mark any that collide with existing names' first letters or syllable shapes -- readers skim; `Kaelen` and `Kaelin` in one cast is a defect.
3. Vet each candidate: say it aloud (no tongue-twisters), search the bible for collisions, quick web check that it isn't a famous trademark or another franchise's signature name.
4. Winner goes into the bible ledger with its culture tag.

## Fanfic names

Match the source universe's naming register instead (wizarding world: Latin-rooted spells, alliterative founders, evocative English surnames). A name that sounds like the wrong franchise breaks immersion faster than any plot hole.

## Titles

- Book titles: promise the tone and the shelf; short enough for a spine.
- Chapter titles: promise the chapter without spoiling its turn; consistent register within a book (all-noun-phrase, all-quotation, etc. -- whichever pattern chapter one set).

## Rules

- Never rename anything already on-page in a published chapter.
- His taste is the tiebreak; present candidates, don't pick for him unless he says "just pick".
