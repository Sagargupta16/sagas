## Rules that always apply

- Sagar's prose is his own. Craft rules inform feedback and drafting help; they never license rewriting his voice.
- Rules are guidance, not dogma: several authors explicitly say to break them (Orwell rule vi, Vonnegut on O'Connor). When a rule fights the story, the story wins -- but say which rule is being broken and why.
- Leonard's minimal-description rules (8-9) conflict with epic-fantasy reader expectations; weight `fantasy-genre.md` over `prose-style.md` for fantasy books.
- Never introduce em/en-dash characters into chapter files; interruption/trailing-off conventions live in `references/dialogue.md` with `--`-safe alternatives.
- Each module ends with a Caveats section listing contested or weakly-sourced items; read it before quoting a rule as fact.
